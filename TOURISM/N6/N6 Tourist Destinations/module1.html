Module 1: Advanced Destination Studies

Part of the N6 Tourist Destinations Study Pack

Created by Karabo Bopape

Unit 1: The Concept of Destination Management

Understand the framework and roles involved in managing tourist destinations.

  • Definition and purpose of Destination Management Organizations (DMOs).
  • Stakeholder roles—government, private sector, communities.
  • Integrated planning: tourism, infrastructure, environment, culture.

Unit 2: Destination Product Development

Learn how tourism products and experiences are created and packaged.

  • Components: attractions, accommodation, transport, amenities.
  • Product lifecycle: from research and design to evaluation.
  • Niche tourism: ecotourism, heritage, MICE (meetings, incentives, conventions, exhibitions).

Unit 3: Market Research & Visitor Profiling

Techniques to understand and segment tourist markets.

  • Primary & secondary data collection methods.
  • Demographic, psychographic, behavioural profiling.
  • Customer satisfaction surveys and feedback analysis.

Unit 4: Brand Positioning & Promotion of Destinations

Strategies for creating and communicating a destination’s identity.

  • Branding elements: identity, logos, slogans.
  • Marketing mix: product, price, place, promotion.
  • Channels: digital, social media, trade fairs, travel agents.

Unit 5: Sustainable Destination Management

Best practices to balance tourism growth with environmental and societal well‑being.

  • Carrying capacity concepts and impact assessments.
  • Community participation and benefit-sharing.
  • Conservation strategies, waste management, cultural heritage protection.

Unit 6: Current Trends in Destination Development

Latest global and local influences shaping destinations.

  • Technology integration: virtual reality, smart tourism, contactless services.
  • Post-pandemic recovery: health measures, agile business models.
  • Local empowerment, slow tourism, experiential travel models.
