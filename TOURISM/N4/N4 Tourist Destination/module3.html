3.1 : Kingdom of Lesotho

Location

Lesotho is a high-altitude, landlocked country entirely surrounded by South Africa. It is the only independent nation in the world that lies entirely above 1,400 meters in elevation. This has earned it the nickname "The Kingdom in the Sky" due to its dramatic mountain landscapes and cool climate.

Lesotho lies in the southeastern part of the African continent and covers a land area of approximately 30,355 square kilometers. Its location within South Africa provides ease of access for tourists but also contributes to close economic and cultural ties with its larger neighbor.

The country is bordered by the following South African provinces:

  • North: Free State Province
  • East and South: KwaZulu-Natal and Eastern Cape Provinces
  • West: North West Province

Lesotho‚Äôs highlands are home to the source of major rivers such as the Senqu (Orange) River, which flows westward through South Africa. The country‚Äôs mountainous geography plays a key role in tourism, agriculture, water supply, and cultural identity.

Capital City

Maseru is the capital and largest city of Lesotho, located along the northwestern border with South Africa, adjacent to the Free State Province. It lies along the Caledon River and serves as the country's administrative, commercial, and political hub.

The city hosts government buildings, foreign embassies, major banks, hotels, shopping centers, and cultural institutions. Maseru reflects a mix of modern infrastructure and traditional Basotho influences, offering visitors both urban experiences and access to nearby rural attractions.

The Moshoeshoe I International Airport (MSU), located approximately 18 kilometers southeast of Maseru, is the main international airport in Lesotho. It connects the country primarily to regional destinations such as Johannesburg in South Africa and serves as the principal air entry point for international tourists.

Maseru is also a gateway to popular tourist destinations such as Thaba-Bosiu, Morija, and the Maloti Route. It offers facilities such as car rentals, travel agencies, local craft markets, and guided tour services.

Climate and Weather

Lesotho has a cool temperate climate due to its high elevation, with most of the country lying above 1,400 meters. As a result, it is significantly cooler than neighboring lowland areas in South Africa. The country's mountainous environment contributes to its appeal as a destination for outdoor and adventure tourism.

The summer season lasts from October to March and brings warm temperatures and frequent afternoon thunderstorms. This is the rainy season, especially in the lowland valleys, where rainfall supports agriculture and replenishes dams and rivers.

The winter season, from May to August, is cold and dry, particularly in the highlands where snowfall is common. Winter temperatures in the mountains can drop below freezing, creating suitable conditions for snow-based activities like skiing at AfriSki Resort.

Spring and autumn serve as transitional periods with moderate temperatures and clearer skies, making them ideal for general sightseeing and cultural travel.

Climate variations between the lowlands and the highlands are important for tourism planning, especially when organizing hiking, camping, or heritage tours. Tourists are advised to pack layered clothing and be prepared for sudden weather changes in the mountains.

Due to its altitude and climate, Lesotho is often referred to as "The Kingdom in the Sky", making it a distinct destination within Southern Africa‚Äôs tourism landscape.

UNESCO World Heritage Site

Lesotho is home to part of the Maloti-Drakensberg Park, a transboundary UNESCO World Heritage Site shared with South Africa. This heritage site is globally recognized for its spectacular mountainous landscapes, exceptional biodiversity, and rich cultural history.

A significant portion of this site lies within Sehlabathebe National Park in southeastern Lesotho. The park features dramatic sandstone formations, alpine flora, rare bird species, and high-altitude wetlands.

One of the park's most valuable cultural assets is its collection of San rock art ‚Äî some of the finest and most extensive examples in Southern Africa. These paintings, created by the indigenous San people, offer insight into their beliefs, lifestyle, and spiritual traditions.

The designation as a UNESCO site helps to protect the area‚Äôs natural and cultural resources, and supports eco-tourism, environmental education, and sustainable development in the region. For tourism students, this site illustrates the importance of conserving heritage for both cultural preservation and international tourism.

Government

Lesotho operates as a constitutional monarchy with a parliamentary system. The country is a sovereign state and follows democratic principles outlined in its national constitution.

The King of Lesotho serves as the ceremonial head of state. While he holds symbolic and traditional significance, the King does not have executive powers. The role is hereditary, representing unity and continuity of the Basotho nation.

The Prime Minister is the head of government and exercises executive authority, including the implementation of laws, policy-making, and administration of national affairs. The Prime Minister is elected from the majority party in the National Assembly.

Lesotho practices a multi-party democracy. The legislature consists of a bicameral Parliament:

  • National Assembly ‚Äì composed of elected representatives from various constituencies.
  • Senate ‚Äì composed of hereditary chiefs and appointed members.

The judiciary is independent and responsible for interpreting laws and ensuring justice. Regular elections are held to promote democratic governance and public accountability.

Understanding Lesotho‚Äôs government structure is important for tourism professionals, as political stability and good governance influence tourism development, international partnerships, and investment.

Lesotho's Economy and Agriculture

Lesotho's economy largely depends on subsistence agriculture, livestock farming, textile manufacturing, and remittances from workers employed in South Africa. Agriculture remains the backbone of the rural economy, with many households engaged in small-scale farming and crop cultivation.

The main crops grown include maize, sorghum, wheat, and barley. These crops are primarily produced for local consumption and food security. Livestock farming is also vital, focusing on sheep, goats, and cattle, which provide meat, milk, wool, and hides.

The textile and garment industry has grown significantly in recent years, becoming a key source of employment and export revenue. This sector benefits from trade agreements such as the African Growth and Opportunity Act (AGOA), which facilitates duty-free access to the US market.

Remittances from Basotho workers employed mainly in South Africa contribute substantially to household incomes and the national GDP, reflecting the close economic and social ties between Lesotho and its neighbour.

In addition, Lesotho is investing in developing other sectors such as tourism, mining, and manufacturing to diversify its economy and create more sustainable growth opportunities.

Accessibility

By Road: Lesotho is accessible via a network of well-maintained roads connecting the country to South Africa. Major border posts include Maseru Bridge, Mohale's Hoek, and Ficksburg, which serve as key entry points for travelers and the transport of goods. Inside the country, the road network links major towns and tourist attractions. However, many mountainous and rural areas require 4x4 vehicles due to rugged terrain and unpaved roads.

By Air: The Moshoeshoe I International Airport, located near Maseru, is Lesotho‚Äôs primary air gateway. It offers regular flights mainly to South African cities such as Johannesburg and Cape Town, providing convenient regional access for tourists, business travelers, and freight services.

By Other Means: While Lesotho does not have major railway lines or seaports due to its landlocked and mountainous geography, road transport remains vital. Domestic air services and helicopter charters are sometimes used for tourism and medical evacuations in remote areas.

Main Tourist Attractions

Lesotho offers a variety of unique natural, historical, and cultural attractions that appeal to both domestic and international tourists. These destinations reflect the country‚Äôs mountainous landscapes, traditional Basotho heritage, and exciting outdoor adventure opportunities.

  • Sehlabathebe National Park: Located in the Maloti Mountains, this UNESCO World Heritage Site features rare alpine flora, bird species, striking sandstone formations, and important San rock art. It is ideal for hikers, bird watchers, and nature lovers.
  • Katse Dam & Botanical Gardens: One of Africa‚Äôs largest dams, Katse Dam is a critical part of the Lesotho Highlands Water Project. The nearby botanical gardens promote the conservation of indigenous plants and support eco-tourism initiatives.
  • Sani Pass & Highest Pub in Africa: A spectacular mountain pass linking Lesotho to KwaZulu-Natal, South Africa. Visitors enjoy breathtaking panoramic views and can visit the Sani Mountain Lodge, home to Africa‚Äôs highest pub at 2,874 meters above sea level.
  • Maletsunyane Falls: One of the highest single-drop waterfalls in Africa at 192 meters, located near Semonkong. The falls are popular for sightseeing, abseiling, and photography.
  • Thaba-Bosiu: A sandstone plateau of great historical and cultural significance. It was the stronghold of King Moshoeshoe I and the birthplace of the Basotho nation.
  • Kome Cave Dwellings: Ancient cave homes carved into rock that are still inhabited today, offering insight into traditional Basotho life and architectural heritage.
  • Dinosaur Footprints: Fossilized dinosaur tracks near Mafeteng and other locations provide important evidence of prehistoric life, making Lesotho a notable paleontological site.
  • San Rock Art: Found in caves across Lesotho, these ancient paintings depict stories of early hunter-gatherer communities and their spiritual beliefs.
  • Tse'hlanyane National Park: A protected park in the Maloti Mountains featuring crystal-clear streams, waterfalls, indigenous forests, diverse wildlife, and scenic hiking trails.
  • Mohale Dam: The second-largest dam in Lesotho, an important part of the national water supply system. It also offers scenic views and opportunities for boating.
  • AfriSki Mountain Resort: The only ski resort in Southern Africa, situated in the Maluti Mountains. It offers skiing, snowboarding, and various mountain adventure activities year-round.
  • Maseru: The capital city, known for its bustling local markets, government buildings, the Royal Palace, and cultural venues.
  • Malealea Lodge & Pony Trek Centre: A community-based tourism lodge offering guided pony treks, cultural tours, and mountain hiking experiences‚Äîan excellent way to explore rural Lesotho and engage with local communities.

Summary: Kingdom of Lesotho

The Kingdom of Lesotho is a small, landlocked country entirely surrounded by South Africa. It is often referred to as the "Kingdom in the Sky" due to its high altitude and mountainous terrain. Lesotho is known for its breathtaking natural landscapes, cultural heritage, and outdoor adventure tourism.

The capital city is Maseru, which serves as the administrative and economic hub. The country operates as a constitutional monarchy with a parliamentary system, where the King is the ceremonial head of state.

Lesotho‚Äôs climate is cool and temperate, with cold, snowy winters in the highlands, making it a popular destination for winter sports. The Maloti and Drakensberg Mountains, along with rivers like the Senqu (Orange) River, define its geography.

The economy relies heavily on agriculture, livestock farming, the textile industry, and remittances from Basotho working in South Africa. Major crops include maize, wheat, and sorghum.

Tourism in Lesotho is growing, supported by a range of accommodations, national parks, heritage sites, and adventure activities such as hiking, pony trekking, and skiing. The country is home to the Maloti-Drakensberg Park, a UNESCO World Heritage Site known for San rock art and biodiversity.

Accessibility is mainly through road connections with South Africa, and by air via Moshoeshoe I International Airport. The country observes several annual cultural events such as the Morija Arts & Cultural Festival and the Lesotho Marathon.

Lesotho provides students and tourism professionals with a valuable case study of a mountainous African destination that blends natural beauty with rich history and culture. 3.2 : Kingdom of eSwatini

Location

eSwatini, formerly known as Swaziland, is a small landlocked country situated in Southern Africa. It is bordered by South Africa on the west, south, and southwest, and by Mozambique to the northeast and east.

  • North and west: Mpumalanga Province of South Africa ‚Äî characterized by its grasslands and part of the Highveld plateau, providing a transition zone between eSwatini and South Africa.
  • South and southeast: KwaZulu-Natal Province of South Africa ‚Äî known for its coastal plains, mountains, and rich Zulu cultural heritage, offering easy access to eSwatini's southern regions.
  • Northeast: Mozambique ‚Äî a coastal country with tropical climates, providing a gateway to the Indian Ocean, influencing regional trade and tourism routes.

eSwatini's location between South Africa and Mozambique positions it as a strategic crossroads for regional trade, culture, and tourism. Its diverse landscapes range from mountainous highlands to lowveld plains, attracting tourists seeking both adventure and cultural experiences.

Capital City

eSwatini has two capital cities with distinct functions. Mbabane is the administrative capital and is located in the northwest of the country in the Highveld region. It hosts most government offices and foreign embassies. Lobamba, situated nearby, is the royal and legislative capital, where the Parliament and Royal Palace are located. The country‚Äôs main international airport is Matsapa International Airport (MTS), situated 8 km from Manzini. It handles international flights and serves as the primary gateway for tourists entering eSwatini by air.

Geo Regions

eSwatini is divided into four main geographical regions: the Highveld, Middleveld, Lowveld, and Lubombo Mountains. These regions vary in elevation, climate, vegetation, and economic activity, and each contributes differently to tourism, agriculture, and settlement patterns.

Highveld: Located in the western part of eSwatini, the Highveld is the highest region, with elevations averaging 1,200 meters. It has a cooler, wetter climate and lush green landscapes. The capital city, Mbabane, is situated here. This region supports forestry, agriculture, and hiking tourism.

Middleveld: Found in the central part of the country, the Middleveld has moderate elevation and transitional climate between the Highveld and Lowveld. It is densely populated and agriculturally productive. The royal capital, Lobamba, is located in this region, making it culturally significant.

Lowveld: This region lies in the east and south and is the hottest and driest area, with elevations below 400 meters. It features savannah vegetation and is used mainly for sugarcane and citrus farming. Several game reserves and wildlife parks are located here, contributing to eco-tourism.

Lubombo Mountains: Running along the eastern border with Mozambique, the Lubombo Mountains are a narrow, rugged mountain range. The climate is warm but more humid due to the altitude. This area is known for scenic beauty, biodiversity, and protected areas like the Mlawula Nature Reserve.

Exam Tip: You may be required to match features or activities with their correct region. Government

eSwatini is an absolute monarchy, one of the few remaining in the world. The King of eSwatini holds supreme executive, legislative, and judicial authority, although some democratic structures exist. The monarch rules in partnership with the traditional Swazi National Council and Parliament. The current monarch is King Mswati III.

Legislative Power: The legislative authority in eSwatini is shared between the King and Parliament. Parliament consists of two chambers: the House of Assembly and the Senate. Some members are elected by the people, while others are appointed by the King. Laws passed by Parliament must be approved by the King before becoming official.

Judicial Power: The judicial system in eSwatini is independent but influenced by traditional and royal authority. It includes a dual system: modern courts (High Court, Court of Appeal) and traditional Swazi courts. The judiciary ensures that justice is applied fairly, but final decisions may be influenced by royal authority in sensitive matters.

Form of Government: eSwatini combines modern political institutions with traditional structures. While there is a Parliament and elections, the King retains strong control over all branches of government. This blend of monarchy and tradition defines the unique political system of the country.

Exam Tip: Examiners often ask about eSwatini‚Äôs form of government or to compare it with other Southern African countries. Remember: eSwatini is an absolute monarchy, with the King playing a central role in all branches of government. Know the roles of Parliament (legislative) and the court system (judicial), including the influence of traditional Swazi law.

Practical Information

  • Currency: The currency used in eSwatini is the Lilangeni (plural: Emalangeni), abbreviated as SZL. The South African Rand (ZAR) is also accepted on a 1:1 basis due to the Common Monetary Area agreement.
  • Entry Requirements: Most tourists require a valid passport to enter eSwatini. Some nationalities may require a visa, depending on bilateral agreements. Visitors should check visa requirements before traveling. Proof of accommodation and sufficient funds may be required at border entry points.
  • Time Zone: eSwatini operates on South African Standard Time (SAST), which is GMT+2. There is no daylight saving time adjustment.
  • Health Requirements: Travelers are advised to be vaccinated against common diseases such as hepatitis A, typhoid, and tetanus. Malaria is present in the Lowveld areas, so antimalarial precautions are recommended. Yellow fever vaccination is required if arriving from a yellow fever endemic country.
  • Languages: The official languages are siSwati and English. siSwati is spoken widely across the country and is used in cultural and traditional settings, while English is used in government, business, and education.
  • Voltage: The electrical voltage in eSwatini is 230 volts, 50 Hz. The power sockets used are of type M and type G. Tourists may need plug adapters and voltage converters for their electronic devices.

Weather Conditions

eSwatini experiences a subtropical climate influenced by regional elevation differences. The country has distinct seasonal changes and a summer rainfall pattern. The western Highveld is cooler and wetter due to its higher altitude, while the eastern Lowveld is much hotter and drier. Rainfall mainly occurs between October and March, while the months from April to September are generally dry.

In the capital city, Mbabane (located in the Highveld), the average temperature during **spring (September to October)** is around **18¬∞C (64.4¬∞F)**. Spring brings a mild increase in warmth and the first rains. During **summer (November to February)**, temperatures rise to between 20¬∞C and 26¬∞C (68¬∞F to 78.8¬∞F), and this is the wettest season with regular afternoon thunderstorms. **Autumn (March to April)** experiences a gradual decrease in rainfall and slightly cooler temperatures, ranging from 16¬∞C to 22¬∞C (60.8¬∞F to 71.6¬∞F). **Winter (May to August)** is dry and cool, with temperatures dropping to 8¬∞C (46.4¬∞F) in the early mornings and rising to about 17¬∞C (62.6¬∞F) during the day.

Exam Tip: Learn the seasonal patterns and regional climate differences‚ÄîHighveld (cooler and wetter), Lowveld (hotter and drier). Also remember: Mbabane is cooler due to its elevation, with a spring average of 18¬∞C. Examiners may ask you to describe climate impacts on tourism or match seasons with weather patterns.

eSwatini's Economy and Agriculture

eSwatini has a mixed economy that relies heavily on agriculture, manufacturing, and services, particularly tourism. The agricultural sector remains vital, employing a large portion of the rural population. Key agricultural products include sugarcane, citrus fruits, maize, cotton, and forestry products. Sugar production is especially important and is one of the country‚Äôs leading exports. Livestock farming is also practiced in rural areas. The manufacturing sector includes food processing, soft drink production, textiles, and wood pulp. Tourism contributes to foreign exchange earnings, especially through cultural tourism, nature reserves, and traditional festivals. eSwatini also benefits from trade relations with neighboring South Africa, its largest trading partner.

Exam Tip: Know the main exports (especially sugar and citrus), and be able to explain how agriculture and tourism support the economy. You may be asked how the country's natural resources and climate support its economic activities or the role of rural farming in the local economy.

Accessibility

By Air: Tourists can access eSwatini by flying into King Mswati III International Airport, located near Sikhuphe in the eastern part of the country. The airport handles international and regional flights, mainly from Johannesburg (South Africa) and Maputo (Mozambique). Charter flights and private aircraft are also accommodated. The airport is modern and equipped with customs, immigration, and transport services for arriving tourists.

By Road: eSwatini is easily accessible by road from both South Africa and Mozambique. The country shares multiple border posts with its neighbors. Major border posts from South Africa include:

  • Bulembu (Josefsdal): Open from 08:00 to 16:00, this border connects the Highveld area to South Africa near Barberton.
  • Gege (Bothashoop): Also open from 08:00 to 16:00, this is a southern entry point from KwaZulu-Natal province in South Africa.
  • Oshoek (Ngwenya): The busiest and most direct route from Johannesburg and Pretoria; open daily from 07:00 to 22:00.
  • Mahamba: Located in the southwest, connecting with Piet Retief in South Africa.
  • Matsamo (Jeppes Reef): Found near the Kruger National Park area, ideal for tourists coming from Nelspruit.
  • Golela Border: Located in the south, this border links with Pongola and northern KwaZulu-Natal.
Roads in eSwatini are well-maintained, and road travel is popular for self-drive tourists and those entering by bus or tour operator vehicles.

By Rail: Although not widely used by tourists, eSwatini has a railway network used mainly for freight. The railway system connects with South Africa and Mozambique, supporting the export of sugar, coal, and wood pulp. Passenger services are limited and not commonly used for tourism purposes.

Exam Tip: Be able to name major entry points into eSwatini, especially the international airport (King Mswati III International Airport) and key border posts like Oshoek/Ngwenya and Bulembu/Josefsdal. Examiners may also ask you to compare travel accessibility by air, road, and rail.

Main Tourist Attractions

eSwatini offers a variety of unique natural, historical, and cultural attractions that appeal to both domestic and international tourists. These destinations reflect the country‚Äôs diverse landscapes, rich Swazi heritage, and opportunities for wildlife viewing and cultural experiences.

  • Ezulwini Valley: Known as the "Valley of Heaven," Ezulwini is located in the Highveld region near Mbabane. It is famous for its beautiful scenery, cultural villages, craft markets, and resorts. Visitors can enjoy hiking, cultural performances, and shopping for traditional crafts.
  • Lobamba: The cultural and royal capital of eSwatini, Lobamba hosts important national ceremonies such as the Umhlanga Reed Dance and Incwala Ceremony. It also houses the National Museum and the Royal Village, offering insight into Swazi traditions and history.
  • Malolotja Nature Reserve: Located in the northwestern Highveld, Malolotja is one of the largest protected areas in eSwatini. It features mountainous terrain, waterfalls, and indigenous forests. Popular activities include hiking, mountain biking, and bird watching.
  • Ngwenya Glass Factory: Situated in Ngwenya, this is Africa‚Äôs oldest glass factory, famous for hand-blown glass products and traditional crafts. Tourists can watch glass blowing demonstrations and purchase unique souvenirs.
  • Milwane Wildlife Sanctuary: The oldest protected area in eSwatini, located near Lobamba. It is home to a variety of animals including antelope, zebras, and bird species. Visitors can enjoy guided walks, game drives, and picnic spots in a natural setting.
  • Phophonyane Falls Nature Reserve: A scenic reserve featuring a series of waterfalls and lush vegetation near the Ezulwini Valley. It offers hiking trails, swimming pools, and opportunities for bird watching.
  • Mkhaya Game Reserve: A private reserve dedicated to protecting endangered species such as the black rhinoceros and white rhinoceros. Located in the Lowveld, it offers guided game drives and walking safaris to see wildlife in a natural environment.
  • Hlane Royal National Park: The largest protected area in eSwatini, located in the Lowveld. It features open savannahs, indigenous forests, and large populations of elephants, lions, and other wildlife. Activities include game drives and bird watching.
  • Malandela's: Located in the Malkerns Valley close to Lobamba, Malandela's is a unique creative venue offering quality food, great shopping, world-class entertainment, and accommodation. It is a cultural hub for locals and tourists alike.
    • House on Fire: A performance area and art gallery featuring the sunken "Afro-Shakespearean Globe Theatre," offering plays, concerts, and cultural events in a distinctive setting.
    • Gone Rural: Produces socially responsible, internationally award-winning handwoven products made from sustainable natural fibers, promoting eco-friendly and ethical crafts.
    • Baobab Batik: A fair trade company offering stunning handmade batik products, including clothing, cushions, and home decor.
    • Zogg's Boutique: Offers quirky and fun gift items handcrafted in eSwatini, perfect for unique souvenirs.
    • Malandela's Farmhouse Restaurant and Country Pub & Malandela's B&B: Provides home-cooked food and unique accommodation experiences in a warm, welcoming environment.
  • Swazi Candle (Mbabane): Founded in 1981 in an old cowshed by two South African graduates, Swazi Candle produces handmade candles where no two are the same. These artisanal products are exported to Europe and North America, supporting local craft industries.
  • Malolotja Nature Reserve: A mountain wilderness on eSwatini's northwestern border with South Africa, the park includes Ngwenya Mountain, the country‚Äôs second highest point at 1,829 meters. Malolotja Falls drops 90 meters, making it the highest waterfall in eSwatini. The reserve offers campsites and is ideal for 4x4 trails, mountain biking, and hiking. It adjoins South Africa's Songimvelo Game Reserve, together forming the Lubombo Transfrontier Conservation Area.
  • Bushman Paintings: Located in Nsangwini in the northern Hhohho region near Chief Molwane Dlamini‚Äôs area, these ancient rock paintings are cultural treasures but are currently damaged and fading. Additional sites include Dlangeni and the Maguga Dam area in Ezulwini Valley, which also features important rock art.

Exam Tip: Be able to name major attractions, their locations, and significance. Know activities available at each site (e.g., hiking in Malolotja, cultural ceremonies in Lobamba, glass blowing in Ngwenya). Summary: Kingdom of eSwatini

The Kingdom of eSwatini is a small, landlocked country located in Southern Africa, bordered by South Africa and Mozambique. Known for its diverse landscapes, from mountainous Highveld to hot Lowveld regions, eSwatini is often celebrated for its rich cultural heritage and natural beauty.

The capital city is Mbabane, the administrative center, while Lobamba serves as the cultural and royal capital. eSwatini is an absolute monarchy ruled by King Mswati III, who holds supreme executive, legislative, and judicial powers alongside traditional and modern governmental institutions.

The climate varies from cool and wet in the Highveld to hot and dry in the Lowveld, with distinct seasons influencing agricultural activities and tourism. Mbabane enjoys an average spring temperature of around 18¬∞C (64.4¬∞F), with rainy summers and dry winters.

eSwatini‚Äôs economy depends on agriculture‚Äîespecially sugarcane, citrus fruits, and forestry‚Äîmanufacturing, and a growing tourism sector that includes cultural festivals, wildlife reserves, and traditional crafts. Trade relations with South Africa are important for economic stability.

Tourism attractions range from natural reserves like Malolotja Nature Reserve and Hlane Royal National Park to cultural events such as the Umhlanga Reed Dance and Incwala Ceremony. Visitors can also explore craft workshops, traditional villages, and game reserves.

Accessibility to eSwatini is mainly via King Mswati III International Airport and several well-established road border posts like Oshoek (Ngwenya) and Bulembu (Josefsdal). Although rail is mostly for freight, road transport is widely used for tourists.

eSwatini observes several important public holidays such as the King's Birthday and Independence Day, which reflect the country's cultural identity and history.
