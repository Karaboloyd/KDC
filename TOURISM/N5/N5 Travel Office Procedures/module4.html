Module 4: Office Planning & Layout

N5 Travel Office Procedures

Developed by Karabo Bopape

1. Office Design Principles

  • Functionality: The layout should suit the nature of work and flow of operations.
  • Flexibility: Must allow for future expansion or reorganization.
  • Safety & Comfort: Adequate lighting, ventilation, and space must be provided.

2. Types of Office Layouts

  • Open Plan: Few barriers; encourages collaboration but may increase noise.
  • Cellular Offices: Separate enclosed offices for individuals or departments.
  • Combination Layout: Mix of open and private offices for balance.

3. Office Furniture & Fixtures

  • Essential Items: Desks, chairs, filing cabinets, shelves, and reception counters.
  • Ergonomics: Choose furniture that supports good posture and movement.
  • Positioning: Arrange to reduce clutter, allow easy movement, and ensure efficiency.

4. Workflow & Space Allocation

  • Departmental Grouping: Similar departments placed close to one another.
  • Work Flow: Logical arrangement of sections for smooth operations (e.g. reception → booking → admin).
  • Space Allocation: Allow enough space for each function, visitor seating, and meeting rooms.

5. Reception Area

  • Function: First point of contact for visitors; must be welcoming and informative.
  • Setup: Comfortable seating, brochures, receptionist desk, branding materials.
  • Professional Image: Clean, neat, organized, and friendly staff presence.

6. Storage & Filing Space

  • Storage Options: Filing cabinets, electronic storage, shelving units.
  • Filing Systems: Alphabetical, numerical, subject-wise, and electronic systems.
  • Security: Confidential documents must be locked and access restricted.

7. Environmental Considerations

  • Lighting: Natural lighting preferred; ensure artificial lighting is adequate.
  • Ventilation: Good air circulation is essential for comfort and health.
  • Noise Control: Use carpets, acoustic panels, and private rooms where needed.
