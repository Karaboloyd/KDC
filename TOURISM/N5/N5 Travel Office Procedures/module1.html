Unit 1.1.1: Define Entrepreneurship

Entrepreneurship is the process of starting and operating a new business venture in order to make a profit. It involves identifying a market opportunity, developing a business plan, and taking on the risks associated with starting a new business. Successful entrepreneurs possess certain characteristics, such as creativity, risk-taking, and strong leadership skills.

Unit 1.1.2: Intrapreneur vs Entrepreneur

An intrapreneur is an employee within a company who is tasked with developing an innovative project or idea, acting like an entrepreneur but without the associated risks. In contrast, an entrepreneur is an individual who starts and runs their own business, taking on all the risks and rewards.

Unit 1.1.3: SKAV in Tourism

  • Skills: Communication, negotiation, and problem-solving
  • Knowledge: Market understanding and industry trends
  • Attitudes: Risk-taking and optimism
  • Values: Ethics and sustainability

Unit 1.1.4: Legal Small Businesses in South Africa

  • Sole Proprietorship: Owned by one person, easy to start
  • Partnership: Two or more owners share profits and risks
  • Private Company (Pty) Ltd: Separate legal entity, limited liability
  • Cooperative: Owned by members for mutual benefit

Unit 1.1.5: Advantages & Disadvantages

  • Sole Proprietorship: Easy setup, but unlimited liability
  • Partnership: Shared skills, but potential disputes
  • Private Company: Limited liability, but costly to set up
  • Cooperative: Shared benefits, but slow decision-making

Basic Accounting Principles

  • Accrual Principle: Record transactions when they happen
  • Consistency Principle: Use same accounting methods
  • Going Concern: Business is assumed to continue operating
  • Matching Principle: Match expenses to income period
