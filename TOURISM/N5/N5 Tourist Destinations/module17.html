Module 1 – Introduction to Entrepreneurship and Accounting Principles

In this module, learners will be introduced to basic entrepreneurial concepts and essential accounting practices relevant to the travel industry. The content includes basic business functions, financial documents, and journals used in travel offices. Learners should understand:

  • The characteristics of successful entrepreneurs
  • The functions of a business (marketing, finance, operations, etc.)
  • Basic accounting documents and procedures
  • How to record financial transactions using CRJ, CPJ, Petty Cash, Ledgers, Trial Balance, and Balance Sheet

1. Cash Receipts Journal (CRJ)

The CRJ is used to record all cash and cheque receipts received by the business.

The CPJ records all cash and cheque payments made by the business.

3. Petty Cash Journal

Petty Cash is used for small payments that are not made by cheque or EFT. The imprest system is typically used.

4. General Ledger

The General Ledger is a collection of all accounts used by a business. Each account contains debit and credit entries.
