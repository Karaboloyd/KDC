Location and Geography

The Maldives is a tropical island nation in the Indian Ocean, located southwest of Sri Lanka and India. It comprises 26 atolls made up of over 1,000 coral islands. It lies near the equator and is known for its crystal-clear lagoons, white sandy beaches, and coral reefs.

Quick Facts

CapitalMalé
Official LanguageDhivehi
CurrencyMaldivian Rufiyaa (MVR)
PopulationApprox. 540,000
Time ZoneGMT +5
ReligionIslam (State religion)
ClimateTropical, with two seasons: dry (northeast monsoon) and wet (southwest monsoon)

Tourist Attractions

  • Malé: The capital city, featuring the Grand Friday Mosque, local markets, and the Maldives National Museum.
  • Maafushi Island: A local island popular for affordable guesthouses and water activities.
  • Banana Reef: One of the oldest and most famous dive sites in the Maldives.
  • Hulhumalé: A reclaimed island offering beaches and local culture close to the airport.
  • Vaadhoo Island: Known for the "Sea of Stars" bioluminescent beach phenomenon.

Activities for Tourists

  • Snorkeling and scuba diving
  • Island hopping
  • Luxury resort stays
  • Sunset cruises
  • Spa and wellness retreats
  • Traditional Maldivian cuisine experiences

Transport and Accessibility

Visitors usually arrive through Velana International Airport (MLE) near Malé. Transfers to resort islands are typically by speedboat or seaplane. Domestic flights and ferries are used for access to local islands.

Travel Tips

  • Respect Islamic customs and dress modestly on local islands.
  • Alcohol is only served on resort islands.
  • Friday is a public holiday; some services may be limited.
  • Best time to visit: November to April (dry season).
