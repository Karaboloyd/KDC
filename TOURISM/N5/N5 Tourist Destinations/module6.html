Unit 6.1: Location

Tanzania is located in East Africa, bordered by Kenya and Uganda to the north, Rwanda, Burundi, and the Democratic Republic of Congo to the west, Zambia, Malawi, and Mozambique to the south, and the Indian Ocean to the east.

Fast Facts:

  • Capital City: Dodoma (official), Dar es Salaam (largest city and major port)
  • Surface Area: Approximately 945,087 sq km
  • Population: About 65 million (2024 estimate)
  • Official Languages: Swahili and English
  • Currency: Tanzanian Shilling (TZS)
  • Independence Day: 9 December 1961

Unit 6.2: Airport Cities, Main Tourist Centres and Principal Transport Routes

  • Main Airports: Julius Nyerere International Airport (Dar es Salaam), Kilimanjaro International Airport (near Arusha).
  • Main Tourist Centres: Serengeti National Park, Mount Kilimanjaro, Zanzibar Island, Ngorongoro Crater.
  • Transport Routes: Well-established road network, railway line connecting Dar es Salaam to inland countries, ferry services to Zanzibar, and ports on the Indian Ocean.

Unit 6.3: Natural Features

Mountains and Parks

  • Mount Kilimanjaro: Africa’s highest mountain and a popular trekking destination.
  • Serengeti National Park: Famous for the Great Migration of wildebeests and predators.
  • Ngorongoro Crater: A UNESCO World Heritage Site with abundant wildlife.
  • Zanzibar Archipelago: Known for its beautiful beaches and historical Stone Town.

Unit 6.4: Form of Government

Tanzania is a republic with a multi-party political system. The President is the head of state and government, elected by popular vote.

Unit 6.6: Climate Conditions

Tanzania has a tropical climate with two rainy seasons: the long rains (March to May) and the short rains (October to December). Coastal areas are hot and humid, while highlands and mountains are cooler.

Unit 6.7: Economy and Agriculture

Tanzania’s economy depends on agriculture (coffee, tea, tobacco, cloves), mining, and tourism. The country is known for its wildlife and natural attractions, drawing visitors worldwide.

Unit 6.8: Accessibility from South Africa

  • By Air: Direct flights from Johannesburg to Dar es Salaam and Kilimanjaro International Airport.
  • By Road: Travel via Botswana, Zambia, and Malawi to Tanzania is possible but long and less common.

Unit 6.9: Tourist Facilities and Services

Tanzania offers a range of accommodation from luxury lodges to budget campsites. Tourist activities include safaris, mountain climbing, cultural tours, and beach holidays.

Unit 6.10: Annual Events

Event Month Description
Serengeti Migration Viewing June – October Seasonal movement of wildebeest and predators, a major attraction.
Zanzibar International Film Festival July Celebrates African culture and arts through film, music, and theater.

Unit 6.11: Shopping in Tanzania

Markets and shops in Dar es Salaam and Stone Town offer crafts like Tanzanite gemstones, Maasai beadwork, carvings, and textiles.

Unit 6.12: Main Tourist Attractions

Natural Attractions

  • Mount Kilimanjaro: Africa’s tallest peak, attracts climbers worldwide.
  • Serengeti National Park: Known for vast savannahs and the Great Migration.
  • Ngorongoro Conservation Area: Large volcanic caldera rich in wildlife.
  • Zanzibar Island: Famous for beautiful beaches and historic Stone Town.

Man-Made Attractions

  • Stone Town: A UNESCO World Heritage site with historic architecture and markets.
  • Museum of Tanzania: Located in Dar es Salaam showcasing natural and cultural history.
