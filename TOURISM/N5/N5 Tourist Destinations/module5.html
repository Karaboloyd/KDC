Unit 5.1: Location

Zambia is a landlocked country located in Southern Africa, bordered by Tanzania to the northeast, Malawi to the east, Mozambique to the southeast, Zimbabwe to the south, Botswana and Namibia to the southwest, Angola to the west, and the Democratic Republic of Congo to the northwest.

Fast Facts:

  • Capital City: Lusaka
  • Surface Area: Approximately 752,612 sq km
  • Population: About 19 million (2024 estimate)
  • Official Language: English
  • Currency: Zambian Kwacha (ZMW)
  • Independence Day: 24 October 1964

Unit 5.2: Airport Cities, Main Tourist Centres and Principal Transport Routes

  • Main Airports: Kenneth Kaunda International Airport (Lusaka), Harry Mwanga Nkumbula International Airport (Livingstone).
  • Main Tourist Centres: Livingstone, Lusaka, South Luangwa National Park, Lower Zambezi National Park.
  • Transport Routes: Good road network connecting major cities; railway links to neighboring countries; major road border posts with Zimbabwe, Botswana, and Mozambique.

Unit 5.3: Natural Features

Rivers and Lakes

  • Zambezi River: One of Africa's longest rivers, forms the southern border with Zimbabwe.
  • Lake Kariba: A large man-made reservoir on the Zambezi River.

National Parks and Wildlife

  • South Luangwa National Park – famous for walking safaris and diverse wildlife.
  • Lower Zambezi National Park – known for canoe safaris on the Zambezi River.
  • Kafue National Park – one of the largest national parks in Africa.

Unit 5.4: Form of Government

Zambia is a republic with a multi-party system. The President is the head of state and government. Zambia holds regular democratic elections.

Unit 5.6: Climate Conditions

Zambia has a tropical climate with a wet season from November to April and a dry season from May to October. Temperatures are warm year-round, cooler in higher altitudes.

Unit 5.7: Economy and Agriculture

Zambia's economy is based on mining (especially copper), agriculture (maize, tobacco, cotton), and tourism. Wildlife safaris are a growing tourist attraction.

Unit 5.8: Accessibility from South Africa

  • By Air: Direct flights from Johannesburg to Lusaka and Livingstone.
  • By Road: Border posts include Beitbridge (to Zimbabwe) and Kazungula (to Botswana and Zambia).

Unit 5.9: Infrastructure: Tourist Facilities and Services

Zambia offers lodges, campsites, and hotels. Tourist services include game drives, walking safaris, river cruises, and cultural tours.

Unit 5.10: Annual Events

Event Month Description
Lusaka Carnival July Cultural celebration with music, dance, and parades.
National Heritage Day First Monday of July Honours Zambia's cultural heritage and independence heroes.

Unit 5.11: Shopping in Zambia

Local markets offer traditional crafts such as baskets, carvings, and textiles. Lusaka’s Arcades and Kabwata markets are popular for souvenirs.

Unit 5.12: Main Tourist Attractions

Natural Attractions

  • Victoria Falls: One of the Seven Natural Wonders of the World, shared with Zimbabwe.
  • South Luangwa National Park: Famous for walking safaris and wildlife viewing.
  • Lower Zambezi National Park: Known for canoe safaris on the Zambezi River.

Man-Made Attractions

  • Lusaka National Museum: Showcases Zambia’s cultural heritage and history.
  • Livingstone Museum: Oldest museum in Zambia, near Victoria Falls.
