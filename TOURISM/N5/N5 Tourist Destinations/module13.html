Location and Geography

Zanzibar is a semi-autonomous region of Tanzania, located in the Indian Ocean. It comprises the main islands of Unguja and Pemba and numerous smaller islands. The region is known for its historic Stone Town, white-sand beaches, and spice plantations.

Quick Facts

CapitalZanzibar City (Stone Town)
Official LanguageSwahili and English
CurrencyTanzanian Shilling (TZS)
PopulationApprox. 1.6 million
Time ZoneGMT +3
ReligionPredominantly Islam
ClimateTropical with two rainy seasons (March–May and November–December)

Tourist Attractions

  • Stone Town: A UNESCO World Heritage Site with rich Swahili culture and architecture.
  • Nungwi and Kendwa Beaches: Known for white sand, turquoise waters, and sunset views.
  • Jozani Forest: Home to the endangered Red Colobus monkey.
  • Prison Island: Famous for its tortoise sanctuary and snorkeling.
  • Spice Tours: Explore clove, nutmeg, and cinnamon farms.

Activities for Tourists

  • Snorkeling and scuba diving
  • Historical and cultural tours
  • Spice plantation visits
  • Dhow cruises and sailing
  • Beach relaxation and local cuisine tasting

Transport and Accessibility

Zanzibar is accessible via ferry or flight from Dar es Salaam, Tanzania. Zanzibar International Airport serves international and domestic travelers. Taxis, minibuses (dala-dalas), and rental cars are available for inland transport.

Travel Tips

  • Respect local customs and dress modestly, especially in Stone Town.
  • Best time to visit: June to October (dry season).
  • US dollars are widely accepted, but local currency is preferred for small purchases.
  • Pack lightweight clothes, insect repellent, and reef-safe sunscreen.
