Unit 7.1: Location

Kenya lies in East Africa, bordering Sudan and Ethiopia to the north, Somalia to the east, Tanzania and Uganda to the west, and the Indian Ocean to the southeast.

Fast Facts:

  • Capital City: Nairobi
  • Surface Area: 580,367 sq km
  • Population: Approximately 50 million (2024 estimate)
  • Official Languages: English and Swahili
  • Currency: Kenyan Shilling (KES)
  • Independence Day: 12 December 1963

Unit 7.2: Airport Cities, Main Tourist Centres and Principal Transport Routes

  • Main Airports: Jomo Kenyatta International Airport (Nairobi), Moi International Airport (Mombasa).
  • Main Tourist Centres: Nairobi, Mombasa, Maasai Mara, Tsavo National Parks.
  • Transport Routes: Good road network, standard gauge railway (SGR) from Mombasa to Nairobi, ferry links to Indian Ocean Islands.

Unit 7.3: Natural Features

Mountains, Lakes and Parks

  • Mount Kenya: Africa’s second‑highest mountain, important for trekking and biodiversity.
  • Maasai Mara: Renowned for the annual Great Migration of wildebeest.
  • Tsavo East & West National Parks: Among the largest parks, famous for elephants and varied wildlife.
  • Lakes Turkana and Nakuru: Key biodiversity sites—Turkana is the world’s largest desert lake, Nakuru is known for flamingo populations.

Unit 7.4: Form of Government

Kenya is a republic with a presidential system. The President is head of state and government, elected by popular vote. The country has a bicameral parliament.

Unit 7.6: Climate Conditions

Kenya has a tropical to temperate climate depending on elevation. Coastal areas are hot and humid; central highlands are cooler; northern regions are arid. Rainy seasons: long rains (Mar–May), short rains (Oct–Dec).

Unit 7.7: Economy and Agriculture

Kenya’s economy includes agriculture (tea, coffee, horticulture), tourism, and services. It also has a growing technology and manufacturing sector.

Unit 7.8: Accessibility from South Africa

  • By Air: Direct flights from Johannesburg to Nairobi and Mombasa.
  • By Road/Rail: Travel via Zimbabwe/Malawi/Tanzania or Zambia unlikely due to long distances; rail link from Mombasa to inland countries.

Unit 7.9: Infrastructure: Tourist Facilities and Services

Kenya offers a wide range of accommodation—from luxury safari lodges and beach resorts to budget campsites and hotels. Tour operators support game drives, cultural experiences, and adventure activities.

Unit 7.10: Annual Events

Event Month Description
Maasai Mara Migration July–October Annual wildebeest migration across the Mara River.
Nairobi International Marathon October International athletics event known for elite long-distance runners.

Unit 7.11: Shopping in Kenya

Nairobi’s Maasai Market, Galleria Mall, and local craft markets offer beaded jewelry, Kisii soapstone carvings, kikoy textiles, and leather goods. Mombasa provides Swahili crafts and dhow souvenirs.

Unit 7.12: Main Tourist Attractions

Natural Attractions

  • Maasai Mara National Reserve: World-famous for wildlife, especially during migration.
  • Mount Kenya: Second-highest peak in Africa, popular for trekking.
  • Tsavo East & West: Large parks with elephants, lions, and red-dust scenery.
  • Lake Nakuru: Known for flamingos and rhino conservation.

Man-Made Attractions

  • Fort Jesus (Mombasa): UNESCO site built by the Portuguese in the 16th century.
  • Nairobi National Museum: Exhibits on Kenya’s cultural and natural heritage.
  • Gedi Ruins: Historic Swahili-Arab settlement near Watamu.
