1.1 Location

Mauritius is an island nation located in the Indian Ocean, east of Madagascar and southeast of the African continent. It is known for its white sandy beaches, lagoons, coral reefs, and volcanic interior.

1.2 How to Get There

Direct flights are available from South Africa via Air Mauritius and other airlines. Sir Seewoosagur Ramgoolam International Airport is the main entry point.

1.3 Tourist Attractions

AttractionDescription
Port LouisCapital city with markets, waterfront, and colonial buildings.

Ile aux CerfsA small island popular for water sports and beach activities.

1.4 Culture and Language

The population is multicultural with Indian, Creole, French, and Chinese heritage. English is the official language, but French and Mauritian Creole are widely spoken.

1.5 Local Cuisine

Famous dishes include dholl puri, rougaille, mine frites, and seafood. Street food is very popular.

1.6 Climate

Mauritius has a tropical climate. Best time to visit is from May to December when it is dry and cool. Cyclone season is from January to March.

1.7 Entry Requirements

South African passport holders do not need a visa for stays up to 90 days. A valid passport, return ticket, and accommodation booking are required.
