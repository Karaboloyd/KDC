Location and Geography

Seychelles is an archipelago of 115 islands in the Indian Ocean, located off the eastern coast of Africa, northeast of Madagascar. It is known for its pristine beaches, coral reefs, and nature reserves, as well as rare animals like the giant Aldabra tortoises.

Quick Facts

CapitalVictoria
Official LanguagesSeychellois Creole, English, French
CurrencySeychellois Rupee (SCR)
PopulationApprox. 100,000
Time ZoneGMT +4
ReligionChristianity (mainly Roman Catholic)
ClimateTropical rainforest climate with high humidity and little temperature variation

Tourist Attractions

  • Beau Vallon: A popular beach known for its clear waters and water sports.
  • Vallée de Mai: A UNESCO World Heritage Site on Praslin Island, home to the rare Coco de Mer palm.
  • La Digue: An island with stunning beaches like Anse Source d’Argent and traditional ox-cart transport.
  • Aldabra Atoll: One of the world's largest coral atolls and a sanctuary for the Aldabra giant tortoise.
  • Morne Seychellois National Park: Great for hiking with panoramic views of Mahé Island.

Activities for Tourists

  • Snorkeling and scuba diving
  • Island hopping
  • Bird watching and eco-tours
  • Creole cuisine tours
  • Fishing and sailing
  • Hiking in nature reserves

Transport and Accessibility

Visitors usually arrive via Seychelles International Airport on Mahé Island. Inter-island transport is available via domestic flights, ferries, and catamarans. Public buses and taxis are available on Mahé and Praslin.

Travel Tips

  • The Seychelles is visa-free for most nationalities for short stays.
  • Pack light cotton clothes, sunscreen, and insect repellent.
  • Tap water is generally safe to drink, but bottled water is widely available.
  • Best time to visit: April to May and October to November for calm seas and pleasant weather.
