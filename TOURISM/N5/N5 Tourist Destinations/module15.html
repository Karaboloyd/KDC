Location and Geography

The Comoros is an archipelago of volcanic islands situated in the Indian Ocean, located at the northern end of the Mozambique Channel between northeastern Mozambique and northwestern Madagascar.

The country consists mainly of three islands: Grande Comore (Ngazidja), Mohéli (Mwali), and Anjouan (Nzwani). The islands are known for their lush vegetation, volcanic peaks, and beautiful beaches.

Tourist Attractions

  • Mount Karthala: An active volcano on Grande Comore, offering hiking opportunities and stunning views.
  • Chomoni Beach: Famous for white sandy beaches and clear waters, ideal for swimming and relaxation.
  • Mohéli Marine Park: A protected area known for marine biodiversity, including coral reefs and turtle nesting sites.
  • Medina of Moroni: A historic town with traditional architecture and vibrant markets.
  • Vanilla and Ylang-Ylang Plantations: Explore fragrant plantations, significant to the local economy and culture.

Activities for Tourists

  • Hiking Mount Karthala and exploring volcanic landscapes.
  • Beach activities: swimming, snorkeling, and sunbathing.
  • Visiting marine parks for diving and wildlife viewing.
  • Exploring traditional markets and cultural tours in Moroni.
  • Visiting spice plantations and learning about local products.
