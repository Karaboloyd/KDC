Unit 1.1: Location of Indian Ocean Islands

The Indian Ocean islands included in this module are:

  • Mauritius
  • Reunion Island (France)
  • Seychelles
  • Madagascar
  • Comoros

These islands are located off the southeastern coast of Africa in the Indian Ocean. They are renowned for their tropical climates, lush scenery, beaches, and biodiversity.

Unit 1.2: Accessibility

Most islands are accessible by direct international flights from Johannesburg or Cape Town. Main airlines include:

  • Air Mauritius
  • Air Seychelles
  • Air Madagascar
  • Kenya Airways
  • South African Airways (limited)

Ferries and cruise ships also connect some of the islands like Mauritius and Reunion.

Unit 1.3: Main Tourist Attractions

Island Main Attractions Activities
Mauritius Grand Baie, Port Louis, Black River Gorges National Park Snorkeling, hiking, shopping, sightseeing
Reunion Island Piton de la Fournaise volcano, beaches, Creole architecture Volcano hiking, helicopter tours, cultural tours
Seychelles Anse Lazio beach, Vallée de Mai, La Digue island Beach relaxation, diving, nature trails
Madagascar Baobab Alley, Nosy Be, Tsingy de Bemaraha Wildlife tours, nature exploration, scuba diving
Comoros Mount Karthala, Moroni, Mayotte beaches Volcano trekking, diving, cultural tours

Unit 1.4: Culture & Languages

Each island has a unique blend of cultures, languages and traditions:

  • Mauritius: English (official), French, Creole
  • Reunion: French, Creole
  • Seychelles: Seychellois Creole, English, French
  • Madagascar: Malagasy, French
  • Comoros: Arabic, French, Comorian

Unit 1.5: Best Time to Visit

The islands are tropical and warm year-round. However, the best travel season is from May to October (cooler and drier). The cyclone season typically occurs between January and March.

Unit 1.6: Travel Tips & Entry Requirements

  • Most islands allow South African passport holders visa-free or visa-on-arrival access for short tourist stays.
  • Local currencies vary: Mauritius Rupee, Seychelles Rupee, Ariary (Madagascar), etc.
  • Health tip: Vaccination against Yellow Fever may be required when arriving from a risk area.
