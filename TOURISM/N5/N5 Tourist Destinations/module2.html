Unit 2.1: Location

Botswana is a landlocked country located in Southern Africa. It lies to the north of South Africa, west of Zimbabwe, south of Zambia, and east of Namibia. Its geographical location places it mainly in the Kalahari Desert region.

Fast Facts:

  • Capital City: Gaborone
  • Surface Area: 581,730 sq km
  • Population: Approximately 2.4 million (2024 estimate)
  • Official Language: English (official), Setswana (widely spoken)
  • Currency: Botswana Pula (BWP)
  • Independence Day: 30 September 1966

Unit 2.2: Airport Cities, Main Tourist Centres and Principal Transport Routes

  • Major Airport: Sir Seretse Khama International Airport in Gaborone.
  • Main Tourist Centres: Gaborone, Maun, Kasane, Francistown, Selebi-Phikwe.
  • Principal Transport Routes: Trans-Kalahari Highway and a well-maintained national road network.
  • Access from South Africa: Via Skilpadshek and Martin’s Drift border posts or flights from Johannesburg/Cape Town.

Unit 2.3: Natural Features

Mountain Ranges and Highlands

  • Tsodilo Hills – UNESCO World Heritage Site with ancient rock art.
  • Matobo Hills – Notable granite formations and cultural history.

Rivers and Wetlands

  • Okavango Delta – One of the largest inland deltas.
  • Limpopo River – Eastern border river.
  • Chobe River – Runs through Chobe National Park.

Deserts

  • Kalahari Desert – Covers most of Botswana with unique desert wildlife.

Lakes and Pans

  • Makgadikgadi Pans – Salt flats important for migratory birds.

Unit 2.4: Form of Government

Botswana is a parliamentary democracy with an elected president. It is known for political stability, low corruption, and strong governance systems.

Unit 2.6: Climate Conditions

Semi-arid climate. Hot summers (Oct–Apr), cool dry winters (May–Sept). Best time to visit: winter/dry season for safaris.

Unit 2.7: Economy and Agriculture

Economy driven by diamonds, tourism, and cattle farming. Botswana has one of Africa’s most stable economies and is a top eco-tourism destination.

Unit 2.8: Accessibility from South Africa

  • By Air: Direct flights to Gaborone from Johannesburg/Cape Town.
  • By Road: Major border posts: Skilpadshek, Martin's Drift.
  • By Rail: Freight rail is available; limited passenger service.

Unit 2.9: Tourist Facilities and Services

Botswana offers luxury safari lodges, guesthouses, domestic flights, and guided tours. Top services available for eco and cultural tourism.
