Unit 3.1: Location

Zimbabwe is a landlocked country in Southern Africa. It borders South Africa, Botswana, Zambia, and Mozambique.

Fast Facts:

  • Capital: Harare
  • Surface Area: 390,757 sq km
  • Population: ~15 million (2024)
  • Languages: English, Shona, Ndebele
  • Currency: Zimbabwean Dollar (ZWL)
  • Independence Day: 18 April 1980

Unit 3.2: Airport Cities, Main Tourist Centres & Transport

  • Airports: Harare (Mugabe Intl), Victoria Falls Airport
  • Tourist Centres: Harare, Bulawayo, Victoria Falls, Hwange NP
  • Transport: Good road & rail links to cities and attractions

Unit 3.3: Natural Features

Rivers & Falls

  • Zambezi: Northern border & Victoria Falls
  • Limpopo: Southern border

Parks & Reserves

  • Hwange NP – largest reserve, elephants
  • Mana Pools NP – UNESCO, canoe safaris
  • Gonarezhou NP – rugged wilderness

Mountains

  • Eastern Highlands – forests & hiking terrain

Unit 3.4: Form of Government

Zimbabwe is a republic with a presidential system and a multi-party political setup.

Unit 3.6: Climate Conditions

Zimbabwe has a subtropical climate: rainy Nov–Mar, dry May–Oct. Best safari time: dry winter.

Unit 3.7: Economy & Agriculture

Main industries: agriculture (tobacco, maize), mining (gold, diamonds), manufacturing, tourism.

Unit 3.8: Accessibility from South Africa

  • By Air: Flights from Johannesburg to Harare & Victoria Falls
  • By Road: Beitbridge border post
  • By Rail: Johannesburg–Bulawayo link, extending to Harare

Unit 3.9: Tourist Facilities & Services

Options include safari lodges, hotels, guesthouses, guided tours, and transport services.

Unit 3.11: Shopping in Zimbabwe

Visit Mbare Musika (Harare) and craft markets near Victoria Falls for beadwork, carvings, textiles.

Unit 3.12: Main Tourist Attractions

Natural

  • Victoria Falls – UNESCO World Heritage
  • Hwange National Park
  • Mana Pools National Park
  • Eastern Highlands

Man-Made

  • Great Zimbabwe Ruins – UNESCO site
  • National Heroes Acre
  • Harare National Gallery
