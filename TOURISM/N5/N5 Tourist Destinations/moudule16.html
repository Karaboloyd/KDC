Unit 8.1: Location

Uganda is a landlocked country in East Africa bordered by Kenya to the east, South Sudan to the north, the Democratic Republic of the Congo to the west, Rwanda to the southwest, and Tanzania to the south.

Fast Facts:

  • Capital City: Kampala
  • Surface Area: 241,038 sq km
  • Population: Approximately 48 million (2024 estimate)
  • Official Languages: English and Swahili
  • Currency: Ugandan Shilling (UGX)
  • Independence Day: 9 October 1962

Unit 8.2: Airport Cities, Main Tourist Centres and Principal Transport Routes

  • Main Airport: Entebbe International Airport (near Kampala)
  • Main Tourist Centres: Kampala, Jinja, Bwindi, Queen Elizabeth National Park
  • Transport Routes: Road connections to Kenya and Rwanda; Lake Victoria ferry routes

Unit 8.3: Natural Features

  • Lake Victoria: Largest lake in Africa; source of the Nile River
  • Rwenzori Mountains: Known as the “Mountains of the Moon” with snow-capped peaks
  • Bwindi Impenetrable Forest: UNESCO site and home to endangered mountain gorillas
  • Murchison Falls: Powerful waterfall on the Nile River

Unit 8.4: Form of Government

Uganda is a republic with a president as head of state and government. It has a multi-party system and a national parliament.

Unit 8.6: Climate Conditions

Uganda has a tropical climate with wet and dry seasons. The country is generally warm with two rainy seasons: March–May and September–November.

Unit 8.7: Economy and Agriculture

The economy relies on agriculture (coffee, tea, fish), tourism, and services. Uganda is known for its eco-tourism and biodiversity.

Unit 8.8: Accessibility from South Africa

  • By Air: Direct flights from Johannesburg to Entebbe International Airport
  • By Road: Overland travel possible but long and complex

Unit 8.9: Infrastructure: Tourist Facilities and Services

Uganda offers eco-lodges, mid-range and luxury safari accommodation, gorilla trekking tours, and cultural village visits supported by local tour companies.

Unit 8.10: Annual Events

Event Month Description
Uganda Martyrs Day June Christian pilgrimage to Namugongo shrine
Nyege Nyege Festival September Music and cultural festival in Jinja

Unit 8.11: Shopping in Uganda

Popular items include bark cloth art, Ankole horn crafts, hand-woven baskets, and traditional drums. Owino Market and Craft Village in Kampala are key spots.

Unit 8.12: Main Tourist Attractions

Natural Attractions

  • Bwindi Impenetrable National Park: Gorilla trekking
  • Queen Elizabeth National Park: Famous for tree-climbing lions and boat safaris
  • Murchison Falls: Stunning waterfall and wildlife viewing
  • Lake Bunyonyi: Scenic lake surrounded by terraced hills

Man-Made Attractions

  • Kasubi Tombs: UNESCO World Heritage Site and royal burial grounds
  • Bahá'í Temple: Only Bahá'í House of Worship in Africa
  • Uganda Museum: Displays of traditional music, history, and culture
