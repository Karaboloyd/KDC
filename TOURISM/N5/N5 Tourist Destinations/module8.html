Unit 8.1: Location

Malawi is a landlocked country in southeastern Africa. It borders Zambia to the west and north, Tanzania to the north and east, and Mozambique on the east, south, and southwest.

Fast Facts:

  • Capital City: Lilongwe
  • Surface Area: 118,484 km²
  • Population: Around 20 million (2024 estimate)
  • Official Languages: English and Chichewa
  • Currency: Malawian Kwacha (MWK)
  • Independence Day: 6 July 1964

Unit 8.2: Airport Cities, Main Tourist Centres and Principal Transport Routes

  • Main Airport: Lilongwe International Airport (Blantyre International Airport secondary).
  • Main Tourist Centres: Lake Malawi, Liwonde National Park, Mulanje Massif.
  • Transport Routes: Tarmac roads connecting major towns; rail line between Blantyre and Mozambique; minibuses common for local routes.

Unit 8.3: Natural Features

Lakes, Rivers & Mountains

  • Lake Malawi: The third largest lake in Africa (560 km long), known for clear waters and unique cichlid fish species.
  • Liwonde National Park: Situated along the Shire River; home to elephants, hippos, crocodiles, and birdlife.
  • Mount Mulanje: A granite massif rising to 3,000 m, featuring hiking and biodiversity.

Unit 8.4: Form of Government

Malawi is a multiparty republic. The President is elected and serves as both head of state and government. The country has active democratic institutional frameworks.

Unit 8.6: Climate Conditions

Malawi has a subtropical climate with a rainy season (November–April) and dry season (May–October). Highland areas like Mulanje are cooler.

Unit 8.7: Economy and Agriculture

Malawi’s economy is based on agriculture — especially tobacco, tea, sugar cane, maize — and tourism centered on Lake Malawi and national parks.

Unit 8.8: Accessibility from South Africa

  • By Air: None direct—travel via Johannesburg to Lilongwe or Blantyre on connecting flights.
  • By Road: Possible via Zimbabwe–Mozambique route though lengthy and challenging.

Unit 8.9: Infrastructure: Tourist Facilities and Services

Malawi offers lodges along Lake Malawi and in parks, budget guesthouses, and camping. Activities include boating, hiking, wildlife safaris, and cultural village visits.

Unit 8.11: Shopping in Malawi

Local markets in Lilongwe and Blantyre sell carvings, woven baskets, textiles, and Lake Malawi craft products (shell crafts, beadwork).

Unit 8.12: Main Tourist Attractions

Natural Attractions

  • Lake Malawi: Offers snorkeling, diving, beaches, and fish diversity.
  • Liwonde National Park: Riverside safaris and abundant wildlife.
  • Mount Mulanje: Popular hiking and biodiversity site.

Man-Made Attractions

  • Capuchin Monastery (Likoma Island): Historic church located on Lake Malawi’s northern island.
  • Ngala Lodge: A popular lakeside resort showcasing Malawi tourism infrastructure.
